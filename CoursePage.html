Courses

Master of Science in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at UC Irvine

A combination of foundational materials science, microfabrication technology, and new topics in semiconductor research.

Frenel zone plate fabricated with electron beam lithography and cryoetching. Image credit: [1].

Fundamentals of Microfabrication

Fall 2025 - Prof. Lawrence Kulinsky

Presents a broad overview of the development of fabrication process to create micro- and nano-scale devices. The discussed techniques range from photolithography, introduced the 1950s for semiconductor fabrication, to ongoing research in MEMS fabrication, such as atomic layer deposition.

Probe station measurement of GFET transfer characteristics, fabricated at M3 Lab.

Nanoscale Fabrication and Characterization

Winter 2026 - Prof. SungWoo Nam

Current research in nano- fabrication and characterization techniques (UV-visible spectroscopy, Raman spectroscopy, near-field scanning optical microscopy) focusing on 2D materials. Lab sessions include demonstrations of graphene field effect transistor (GFET) fabrication [2], [3].

Schematic of Riber 32 system for oxide MBE growth of lead titanite-based thin films, e.g., PZT. Image credit [4].

Semiconductor Manufacturing

Winter 2026 - Prof. Qi Song

An introduction to the physics of and techniques for thin film synthesis. Discussed topics include plasma generation, physical and chemical vapor deposition, vacuum system design, and molecular beam epitaxy (MBE).

EDS elemental analysis of high dielectric metal transistor gate. Image credit: [5].

Transmission Electron Microscopy

Spring 2026 - Prof. Xiaoqing Pan

Introduction to the theory and operation of electron microscopes. Includes laboratory demonstrations of electron diffraction, energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDS), electron energy loss spectroscopy (EELS), and scanning transmission electron microscopy (STEM) imaging.

Fabrication steps for a silicon micro-diaphragm using the PiezoMUMPS process.

Microsystem Design

Spring 2026 - Prof. Camilo Velez Cuervo

Students design, analyze, and simulate a chosen microsystem. The lumped-element model, COMSOL simulations, and mask design using L-edit are introduced. Microfabriation techniques are discussed, with students developing a fabrication process compatible with multi-user MEMS processes (MUMPS).
