What is the Dual-Shell Gyroscope?

The Dual-Shell gyroscope (DSG) is a microscale fused silica resonator gyroscope. Developed at the UCI MicroSystems Lab, the DSG uses micro-glassblowing to create a high-performance sensor used for navigation where access to a GPS signal is limited.

Fabrication

Cavities are defined in the fused silica wafer

A combination of lithography, RIE dry etching, and HF wet etching is used to form cavities in the wafer.

A triple stack is formed

Oxygen plasma is used to activate the surface of the wafer, making it hydrophilic. Then, the wafers are aligned and bonded together when placed in direct contact.

3D geometry is created by glassblowing

Air trapped inside the cavities expands at 1700C, forming the inner and outer shells of the DSG. This method allows the formation of highly symmetric micro-scale devices.

Release of the vibrating element

Back-lapping planarizes and removes material connecting the 2 shells. The vibrating inner shell is responsive to angular acceleration.
