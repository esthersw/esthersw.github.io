Thesis Project

INRF Graduate Fellow at UC Irvine

Becoming a graduate fellow at the Integrated Nanosystems Research Facility (INRF) allowed me to access class 100/1,000/10,000 cleanrooms. I gained an engineer's perspective by becoming involved in equipment verification and validation, getting a glimpse behind the user interface of these specialized tools.

The directionality of a vapor cloud changes with the thermal evaporation source.

Film Uniformity

The Angstrom deposition system at INRF combines resistive heating for thermal evaporation, electrom beam evaporation, and ion beam milling in one chamber. I conducted an analysis of film uniformity that included analytical modeling based on [1] and experimental measurement, ensuring high quality of deposited films.

References
[1] M. Ohring, The Materials Science of Thin Films, 1st ed. San Diego, CA: Academic Press, 1992.
